## Judging Criteria

1. **Original and interesting ideas** — judges reward genuine intellectual contribution
2. **Quality of argument** — clear structure, evidence-backed claims, logical reasoning
3. **Quality of writing** — language, flow, engagement
4. **Research depth** — proper academic sources, MLA 8 citations
5. **No plagiarism / No AI** — automatic disqualification

Judging panel: scholars from Harvard, MIT, Stanford, Cambridge, Oxford — **double-blind review**.

## The 9 Prompts — Senior Division 2026

Students choose **ONE** prompt only.

| # | Prompt | Theme |
|---|--------|-------|
| 1 | Is populism inevitably a destructive force in democracy, or can it be reimagined as a positive force for change? | Politics / Democracy |
| 2 | Can the nation-state survive the pressures of globalisation, climate change, and forced migration? | IR / Political Theory |
| 3 | Should AI systems have legal personhood? | AI / Law / Ethics |
| 4 | Should colonial-era artifacts in Western museums be repatriated to their countries of origin, or preserved where they are as part of global heritage? | History / Ethics |
| 5 | Can meritocracy and structural inequality coexist, or does one inevitably undermine the other? | Sociology / Economics |
| 6 | Are open borders morally justified? | Ethics / Migration |
| 7 | Should the pursuit of scientific knowledge be constrained by ethical considerations? | Philosophy of Science |
| 8 | Should some technologies be restricted even if they could eliminate disease or extend human life? | Bioethics / Technology |
| 9 | What does it mean to heal? | Philosophy / Medicine / Culture |

## Prompt Selection Strategy

**Strongest for a 14–18 student with good research access:**
- **Prompt 4** (Colonial artifacts) — concrete examples, rich academic literature, clear sides
- **Prompt 3** (AI legal personhood) — highly topical, strong professor reading list provided
- **Prompt 6** (Open borders) — clear moral philosophy framework, accessible evidence

**Highest ceiling for originality:**
- **Prompt 9** (What does it mean to heal?) — interdisciplinary, creative, less crowded
- **Prompt 5** (Meritocracy) — nuanced, allows personal angle

**Most competitive / most submissions expected:**
- **Prompt 1** (Populism) — very topical but many students will write on this
- **Prompt 2** (Nation-states) — broad, risk of superficiality
