## WHAT TO LOOK FOR IN A MENTOR

When evaluating any mentorship program, ask:

1. **Does the mentor have economics expertise?** (PhD in economics or related field)
2. **Have they helped students with essay competitions specifically?** (Not just university admissions)
3. **Can they provide references from past students?**
4. **What is their feedback process?** (Multiple rounds of revision?)
5. **Do they understand the John Locke Institute style?** (Philosophical, argumentative, not just descriptive)

---

## RED FLAGS TO AVOID

- Organizations that write essays *for* students (academic dishonesty)
- Programs that promise guaranteed wins
- Mentors without relevant academic credentials
- Programs that don't provide individualized feedback
- Very short turnaround programs (good mentorship takes time)

---

## WHAT GOOD MENTORSHIP LOOKS LIKE

A good mentor for JLI will:
- Help you **develop your own argument**, not give you theirs
- Push back on weak reasoning
- Suggest academic sources you haven't found
- Review multiple drafts with specific feedback
- Help you understand the counterarguments
- Know the JLI judging criteria
