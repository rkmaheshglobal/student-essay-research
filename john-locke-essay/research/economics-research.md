## About the John Locke Institute Essay Competition

The John Locke Institute Essay Competition is one of the most prestigious academic essay competitions for pre-university students worldwide. It challenges students to engage with complex questions across multiple disciplines including Economics, Philosophy, Politics, History, Psychology, Theology, and Law.

### What Judges Look For:
1. **Original thinking** - Not regurgitating textbook answers
2. **Clear argumentation** - Logical structure with well-supported claims
3. **Engagement with counterarguments** - Acknowledging and addressing opposing views
4. **Evidence-based reasoning** - Using data, case studies, and academic sources
5. **Writing quality** - Clear, concise, and sophisticated prose

## Economics Essay Questions - Typical Themes

The John Locke Institute typically releases 3-4 economics questions each year. Based on historical patterns, questions often explore:

### 1. Market Mechanisms & Efficiency
- Market failures and government intervention
- Price mechanisms and resource allocation
- Externalities and public goods

### 2. Inequality & Distribution
- Wealth and income inequality
- Redistribution policies
- Intergenerational mobility

### 3. Monetary & Fiscal Policy
- Central bank independence
- Inflation targeting
- Government debt and deficits

### 4. International Economics
- Trade policy and protectionism
- Globalization effects
- Currency and exchange rates

### 5. Behavioral Economics
- Rationality assumptions
- Nudge theory and paternalism
- Decision-making biases

### 6. Development Economics
- Poverty alleviation strategies
- Aid effectiveness
- Institutional economics

### 7. Environmental Economics
- Carbon pricing mechanisms
- Sustainability and growth
- Climate change economics

## Research Topics to Explore

### Topic 1: The Economics of Artificial Intelligence

**Why This Matters:**
- AI is transforming labor markets globally
- Raises questions about productivity, inequality, and the future of work
- Highly relevant and timely topic

**Key Questions to Explore:**
- Will AI increase or decrease inequality?
- How should governments respond to AI-driven job displacement?
- What is the economic value of AI-generated content?

**Key Sources:**
- Acemoglu & Restrepo (2019) - "Automation and New Tasks"
- Brynjolfsson & McAfee - "The Second Machine Age"
- OECD reports on AI and employment

---

### Topic 2: Universal Basic Income (UBI)

**Why This Matters:**
- Growing interest due to automation concerns
- Multiple pilot programs worldwide
- Challenges traditional welfare economics

**Key Questions to Explore:**
- Is UBI economically feasible?
- What are the labor supply effects?
- How does UBI compare to targeted welfare?

**Key Sources:**
- Finland UBI experiment results
- GiveDirectly studies in Kenya
- Milton Friedman's negative income tax proposal

---

### Topic 3: Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs)

**Why This Matters:**
- Major central banks exploring digital currencies
- Could transform monetary policy transmission
- Implications for financial stability

**Key Questions to Explore:**
- Should central banks issue digital currencies?
- What are the privacy implications?
- How would CBDCs affect commercial banking?

**Key Sources:**
- BIS reports on CBDCs
- China's digital yuan pilot
- ECB digital euro project

---

### Topic 4: Carbon Pricing and Climate Economics

**Why This Matters:**
- Climate change is the defining challenge of our time
- Economic instruments are key policy tools
- Debates about efficiency vs. equity

**Key Questions to Explore:**
- Carbon tax vs. cap-and-trade: which is more effective?
- How should carbon revenues be distributed?
- Can economic growth be decoupled from emissions?

**Key Sources:**
- Nordhaus (Nobel Prize work on climate economics)
- EU Emissions Trading System data
- Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism debates

---

### Topic 5: The Economics of Healthcare

**Why This Matters:**
- Healthcare spending is a major economic issue
- Market failures are prevalent in healthcare
- COVID-19 highlighted system vulnerabilities

**Key Questions to Explore:**
- Is healthcare a public good?
- How should pharmaceutical innovation be incentivized?
- What explains healthcare cost variations across countries?

**Key Sources:**
- Arrow (1963) - "Uncertainty and the Welfare Economics of Medical Care"
- OECD Health Statistics
- Comparative health system studies

---

### Topic 6: Degrowth vs. Green Growth

**Why This Matters:**
- Fundamental debate about economic paradigms
- Challenges conventional economic thinking
- Highly philosophical and provocative

**Key Questions to Explore:**
- Is perpetual economic growth possible or desirable?
- Can technology solve environmental limits?
- What would a post-growth economy look like?

**Key Sources:**
- Kate Raworth - "Doughnut Economics"
- Tim Jackson - "Prosperity Without Growth"
- World Bank green growth reports

## Essay Writing Strategy

### Phase 1: Topic Selection (Week 1-2)
1. Wait for official 2026 questions to be released (April 1, 2026)
2. Evaluate each question against your interests and knowledge
3. Choose the question where you can offer the most original perspective

### Phase 2: Research Deep Dive (Week 2-4)
1. Read foundational academic papers
2. Gather current data and statistics
3. Identify 2-3 case studies to support arguments
4. Map out counterarguments

### Phase 3: Outline & Draft (Week 4-6)
1. Create detailed outline with thesis statement
2. Write first draft focusing on argument flow
3. Ensure each paragraph advances the argument

### Phase 4: Revision & Polish (Week 6-8)
1. Get feedback from mentors/teachers
2. Strengthen weak arguments
3. Refine prose and eliminate redundancy
4. Final proofread

## Recommended Reading List

### Foundational Economics
1. **"Economics" by Paul Samuelson** - Classic textbook
2. **"Thinking, Fast and Slow" by Daniel Kahneman** - Behavioral economics
3. **"Capital in the Twenty-First Century" by Thomas Piketty** - Inequality

### Contemporary Issues
4. **"Good Economics for Hard Times" by Banerjee & Duflo** - Policy debates
5. **"The Deficit Myth" by Stephanie Kelton** - Modern Monetary Theory
6. **"Narrative Economics" by Robert Shiller** - How stories drive economies

### Essay Writing
7. **"The Elements of Style" by Strunk & White** - Writing fundamentals
8. **"They Say / I Say" by Graff & Birkenstein** - Academic argumentation
